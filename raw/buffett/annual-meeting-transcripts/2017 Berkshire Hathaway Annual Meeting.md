2017 Annual Meeting 2017年年会

(May 6, 2017) A series of scandals at Wells Fargo has Buffett defending Berkshire’s big stake in the bank. He also makes the case for his Coca-Cola habit, explains why he changed his mind on the airline stocks he once called a “death trap,” criticizes “financial helpers,” and reveals the stock he was “too dumb” to even consider buying.  
（2017年5月6日）富国银行的一系列丑闻迫使巴菲特出面捍卫伯克希尔哈撒韦公司在该银行的大量股份。他还解释了自己为何钟爱可口可乐，阐述了为何改变了对曾经被他称为“死亡陷阱”的航空公司股票的看法，批评了“金融助手”，并透露了他“愚蠢到”甚至都没考虑过的股票。

Berkshire Hathaway Shareholder Letters —  
伯克希尔哈撒韦公司致股东信——

Berkshire’s 10.7 percent increase in per-share book value lagged the S&P’s 12.0 percent advance, but its stock price gain of 23.4 percent easily outperformed the index. In his annual letter, Buffett wrote about winning a 10-year wager that a low-cost index fund would outperform a large group of high-fee hedge funds. His bottom line: “It will usually be the managers who reap outsized profits, not the clients.”  
伯克希尔哈撒韦公司每股账面价值增长10.7%，低于标普500指数12.0%的涨幅，但其股价涨幅高达23.4%，轻松跑赢大盘。巴菲特在年度致股东信中写道，他打了个赌，赌一只低成本指数基金的表现会优于一大批高收费对冲基金，并最终赢得了这场为期十年的赌局。他的结论是：“通常情况下，获得超额利润的是基金经理，而不是客户。”
